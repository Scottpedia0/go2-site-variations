The offer

A simple 3-step start.

Step 01

Book a call.

We talk about your team, your goals, the workflow you want to improve, and the questions you want the audit to answer.

Step 02

Install on one machine.

We install the Go2 desktop agent on any worker machine you choose. It runs for 1-2 weeks and captures the workflow signal locally.

Step 03

Get your audit.

We send you a ranked operational audit for that role and workflow: findings, confidence scores, automation opportunities, and the real tools, loops, and workarounds shaping the job.

What the pilot actually is: we instrument one real role, send back a ranked audit, show you what to fix or automate, and in some cases even write automations for you based on your existing tool stack. Then you can decide what you want to do from there.

Privacy and handling: raw workflow data stays private and on the worker’s machine. Processing is handled with ZDR, and if you need stricter handling or a customer-managed environment, we can talk through that on the call.

FAQ

Short answers to the obvious questions.

What is Go2, in plain language?

Go2 looks at how work actually happens instead of relying on docs, SOPs, or memory. It turns workflow telemetry into useful findings first, then into better decisions about what should be fixed, standardized, or automated.

What stays private and how is it handled?

The raw workflow data stays private and on the worker’s machine. Processing is handled with ZDR, and if you need tighter controls or a customer-managed environment, we can structure around that.

What do we actually get back?

You get back a ranked operational audit: findings, confidence scores, automation opportunities, and a better read on the tools, workarounds, and loops actually shaping the job.

What makes this different from another AI tool pitch?

Most AI tools start with docs, prompts, or a cleaned-up version of the process. Go2 starts with the work as it actually happens, which gives you a much better shot at creating measurable ROI instead of just adding another layer of software.

What happens if it is a fit?

If the audit is useful, you can implement the changes yourself, have us help with light implementation, or expand it into a broader team audit and implementation path.
