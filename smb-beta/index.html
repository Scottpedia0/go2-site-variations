FAQ

Short answers to the obvious questions.

What is Go2, in plain language?

Go2 is software that looks at how work actually happens instead of relying on docs, SOPs, or memory. It turns that real workflow signal into worker help first, then process insight, then a much better starting point for automation.

What does it actually look at?

It sees enough of the workflow on the machine to understand patterns, repeats, handoffs, tool switching, and the places where work gets stuck. It has to. Otherwise the insight is fake.

That raw signal is not enough by itself, which is why the worker adds the missing context. That is how you get closer to the truth of the job instead of a clean but useless process story.

What do workers get out of it?

Workers should get something useful quickly. That might be a cleaner way to batch work, avoid repeated steps, use a better prompt, tighten a handoff, or stop wasting time on the same workaround every day. If that part is not useful, the whole thing falls apart.

What do leaders get out of it?

You get a less fake picture of what is actually happening in the workflow. That makes it easier to see what should be fixed, standardized, or automated, and what will break if you try to force AI into a process that is still messy underneath.

Why is this different from the AI tools everyone keeps pitching?

Most of those start with docs, prompts, or a knowledge base. The problem is that most real operational knowledge is not sitting neatly in one place. It is in DMs, notes, tickets, side conversations, copied fields, approvals that sit, and workarounds people stop noticing.

That is why AI on top of docs so often looks smart and then breaks in the real workflow. Go2 starts with the work itself.

Why are you opening this beta now?

Because we already know this signal is valuable. We have seen it across billions of workplace events and worker installs. What we want to tighten now is the SMB rollout so it works cleanly without a heavy enterprise wrapper around it.

What about privacy, security, or local-only requirements?

There are a few ways to handle storage and processing depending on the business. If you need tighter controls, local-only handling, or something specific because of customer data or IP, put that in the intake. We will talk through what is realistic.

What if this is not a fit?

Then we should tell you that directly. You should still leave the call with a better read on the workflow and some practical next steps.
