🤖 What is an AI agent, exactly?
An AI agent is like a smart assistant that connects to your tools and runs tasks for you automatically. You tell it what you want in plain English — "every Monday morning, read my calendar, pull KPI data from Sheets, and draft 1:1 agendas for each of my direct reports" — and it does the work across Google Sheets, Calendar, Gmail, Meet, and Drive without you having to touch any of it. You don't need to be a developer. You don't need to write code. You need a few clear instructions, and the agent handles the execution. Think of it as building a small AI team that handles your admin while you handle your people.
Priya's workflow has four recurring administrative cycles that consume most of her non-meeting time: Monday morning prep (reading the week, pulling KPI status, drafting agendas), post-meeting summaries (capturing what happened, extracting action items, updating docs), weekly reporting (compiling team performance, account status, hours), and performance review drafting (pulling scores, pulling 1:1 notes, writing narratives). Each of these is a structured, repeatable task — exactly the kind of thing an AI agent can handle end to end.

The difference between what Priya does today (using Gemini manually for each of these, starting from scratch each time) and what she could have (agents that handle these workflows automatically before she even sits down) is the difference between doing admin and reviewing admin. She becomes the editor, not the assembly line.

What it does — step by step
1
Enable transcription in Google Meet — go to meet.google.com → Settings → Recording & Transcripts → turn on "Transcription". This is free for Google Workspace accounts. When enabled, every meeting generates a transcript automatically saved to your Google Drive.
2
Transcript lands in Drive — after your 1:1 ends, the transcript appears in a folder called "Meet Recordings" in your Drive. It's a Google Doc with a timestamped conversation log.
3
Agent reads the transcript — you (or a scheduled script) opens the new transcript doc and passes it to Gemini or Claude with the summary prompt below.
4
Agent generates the structured summary — Discussion, Mood & Engagement, Action Items (with owner and due date), Follow-ups for next 1:1.
5
Summary appended to the 1:1 doc — the agent writes the summary into the correct Google Doc for that person, tagged with the date. Your records stay current with zero manual writing.
6
Action items extracted to your task list — any action item tagged to Priya goes into Google Keep as a task. Action items for the report are noted in the summary doc.

Step-by-Step Setup (one time, 5 minutes)
1
Enable Meet Transcription: In Google Meet, click the three dots (More options) → Settings → Recording & Transcripts → toggle on "Transcription." Or ask your Google Workspace admin to enable it for your account. It's free with most Workspace plans.
2
Start transcription before your next 1:1: When you join the meeting, click Activities (the grid icon) → Transcripts → Start Transcription. A banner will appear for all participants saying the meeting is being transcribed.
3
After the meeting: The transcript saves automatically to your Google Drive in a folder called "Meet Recordings." Open it — it's a Google Doc with a timestamped conversation log.
4
Open Gemini and paste Prompt 1B above. Copy the transcript from the Drive doc and paste it in. You'll have a complete structured summary in under 2 minutes.
5
Copy the summary into your 1:1 Google Doc for that person (one doc per report, all their summaries in chronological order). You're done. No manual notes, no reconstruction from memory.
6
Optional — build the Gemini Gem (see Tool 4 below) for the Post-Meeting Summary so you never have to paste the prompt again. Open the gem, paste the transcript, done.
Time math: You have 8 weekly 1:1s. Each post-meeting write-up currently takes 30–45 minutes. With Meet transcription + this prompt: 90 seconds per summary. That's approximately 4 hours recovered every single week — just from this one change.
