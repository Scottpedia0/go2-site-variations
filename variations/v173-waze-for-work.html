The Precedent

How Waze proved the model

The Waze story isn't just an analogy. It's a proven playbook for turning crowdsourced data into massive value.

2008

Launch: The data is invisible

GPS devices had maps, but no real-time traffic data. Drivers had no way to share what they saw. The data existed in millions of phones but wasn't being captured.

2009

Capture: Passive data collection begins

Waze's app runs in the background. No effort required. Just drive. The phone captures speed, route, and stops automatically.

2011

Network effect: Critical mass reached

With millions of users, Waze's maps became more accurate than Google's. Real-time data beats static maps every time.

2013

Exit: Google acquires for $1.1B

The data asset was so valuable that Google paid $1.1 billion. Not for the app. For the crowdsourced data network.

2026
