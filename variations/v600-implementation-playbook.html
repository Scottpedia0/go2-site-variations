Phase 1: Pilot Program

Weeks 1-4 • 15-25 volunteers • 1 department

Start small with volunteers. Prove value, build internal champions, and refine your approach before expanding.

Week 1 — Setup & Launch

Technical Setup & Team Onboarding

  • ☐Install Go2 agent on pilot group machines (15-25 people)
  • ☐Configure privacy settings and data policies per your org requirements
  • ☐Run 30-minute orientation session: what Go2 does, what it doesn't, how data is used
  • ☐Set up manager dashboards for pilot team leads
  • ☐Establish baseline metrics before coaching begins
Week 2 — Baseline & Observation

AI Learns Your Patterns

  • ☐Go2 AI observes and categorizes work patterns (no coaching yet)
  • ☐Review auto-categorization accuracy — make adjustments
  • ☐Pilot participants check personal dashboards and give feedback
  • ☐Address any questions or concerns from pilot group
Weeks 3-4 — Coaching Begins

First Insights & Coaching Recommendations

  • ☐Enable AI coaching for pilot group
  • ☐Individuals receive personalized productivity recommendations
  • ☐Managers receive team-level optimization suggestions
  • ☐Conduct mid-point survey (satisfaction, value perception)
  • ☐Compare pilot group metrics to baseline
  • ☐Document wins, concerns, and lessons learned

Phase 1 Success Criteria (Go/No-Go for Phase 2)

✓80%+ pilot participants report positive experience
✓10%+ measurable productivity improvement
✓Zero privacy or security concerns unresolved
✓3+ internal champions identified for Phase 2

Phase 2: Department Rollout

Weeks 5-8 • Full department (50-100 people) • 2-3 teams

Expand to full departments using pilot champions as advocates. Refine policies and build organizational momentum.

Week 5 — Expand

Department-Wide Deployment

  • ☐Deploy to 2-3 full departments (50-100 people)
  • ☐Pilot champions run peer onboarding sessions
  • ☐Configure department-specific policies and categories
  • ☐Set up SSO integration (Okta, Azure AD, Google)
Weeks 6-7 — Optimize

Refinement & Cross-Team Insights

  • ☐Enable cross-team analytics for department heads
  • ☐AI generates team-level coaching and workflow recommendations
  • ☐Integrate with existing tools (Slack notifications, calendar sync)
  • ☐Run manager training on data-informed coaching conversations
Week 8 — Validate

Measure & Prepare for Company-Wide

  • ☐Comprehensive ROI report for Phase 2 departments
  • ☐Employee satisfaction survey (target: 85%+ positive)
  • ☐Executive presentation with results and Phase 3 plan
  • ☐Finalize company-wide policies and communication plan

Phase 2 Success Criteria

✓85%+ employee satisfaction across expanded group
✓Measurable ROI documented and presented to leadership
✓SSO and security integrations complete
✓Executive sponsorship confirmed for company-wide
3

Phase 3: Company-Wide

Weeks 9-12 • All employees • Full organization

Roll out to the entire company with proven playbook, trained managers, and built-in champions on every team.

Weeks 9-10 — Deploy

Organization-Wide Rollout

  • ☐Mass deployment via MDM/SCCM or self-service install portal
  • ☐Company-wide communication from executive sponsor
  • ☐Department champions run localized onboarding sessions
  • ☐IT support SOP published for common questions
Weeks 11-12 — Stabilize & Optimize

Full Optimization & Ongoing

  • ☐Enable company-wide executive dashboards
  • ☐Establish quarterly review cadence with Go2 Customer Success
  • ☐Set up automated weekly insight digests for all managers
  • ☐Document institutional knowledge and ongoing best practices
  • ☐Celebrate wins and share success stories internally

Phase 3 Success Criteria

✓95%+ deployment coverage
✓42%+ productivity improvement (Go2 average)
✓Executive dashboard delivering weekly insights
✓Ongoing quarterly review process established

Common Risk: "Big Brother" Perception

The #1 risk to adoption is employees feeling surveilled. Mitigate this by: (1) leading with individual benefit — employees see their own data first, (2) using pilot champions as peer advocates, (3) transparent communication about exactly what is/isn't tracked, (4) positioning as coaching, never monitoring. Companies that follow this playbook achieve 98% adoption rates.
