The Pattern

Anatomy of Quiet Quitting

Disengagement doesn't happen overnight. It follows a pattern. Go2 sees each stage.

Week 1-2: Subtle Shift

Collaboration Changes

They stop volunteering for cross-team projects. Slack messages become shorter. They respond but don't initiate. It looks like nothing. The data sees everything.

Go2 detects: -12% collaboration score
Week 3-4: Routine Withdrawal

Reduced Initiative

They do what's asked but nothing more. No extra ideas in meetings. No late-night debugging sessions (the passion ones, not the forced ones). They're present but not invested.

Go2 detects: -23% initiative patterns, scope narrowing
Week 5-6: Visible Disengagement

Work Pattern Changes

Sessions become shorter. More frequent breaks. Tool usage narrows to minimum requirements. Calendar starts clearing as they decline optional meetings.

Go2 detects: -31% engagement score, work session duration -18%
Week 7-8: Active Checking Out

Minimum Viable Effort

They meet deadlines but barely. Quality subtly drops. They stop attending optional events. Their network within the company contracts. They're doing the job, just barely.

Go2 detects: -42% engagement, network contraction, quality metrics declining
Week 9-10: Point of No Return

Resignation Imminent

By this point, they've already updated their LinkedIn. They're interviewing. Their remaining engagement is purely transactional. Without intervention weeks ago, this was inevitable.

Traditional detection: manager notices "something seems off"

This Isn't About Catching People

Go2 doesn't detect quiet quitting to punish it. It detects disengagement to understand and address it. The difference is everything.

The Wrong Response

Surveillance & Punishment

"We noticed you're less active. You're on a performance improvement plan." This accelerates departure and destroys remaining trust.

More Monitoring

Installing keystroke loggers and screenshot tools. The exact opposite of what a disengaged employee needs. Guarantees they leave faster.

Ignoring It

Hoping it resolves itself. It never does. Disengagement is a signal, not a phase. Without action, it either becomes resignation or spreads to the team.

The Right Response

Curiosity & Conversation

"I noticed some patterns that suggest you might be dealing with something. How can I help?" Start from care, not accusation.

Address Root Causes

Go2 data often reveals the WHY: meeting overload, unclear growth path, burnout recovery, role misfit. Fix the cause, not the symptom.

Systemic Improvement

If disengagement patterns appear across a team, the problem isn't the people, it's the environment. Go2 identifies systemic issues before they become culture problems.
