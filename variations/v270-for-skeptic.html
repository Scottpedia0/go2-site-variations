What Go2 can't do.
Because any company that won't tell you their limitations is lying about their capabilities.

It takes 1-2 weeks to be useful.

Go2 needs to observe your processes before it can coach on them. The first 48 hours give you initial insights, but the real value comes at week 2-3 when pattern recognition kicks in. If you need answers today, this isn't your tool.

It doesn't work for pure physical labor.

Go2 analyzes computer-based work. If your employees work in a warehouse, on a factory floor, or in the field with no computer interaction, Go2 won't help. We won't pretend otherwise.

It won't catch slackers.

If your goal is surveillance -- catching people watching YouTube or measuring exact minutes worked -- use Time Doctor. That's not what Go2 does. We optimize processes, not police people.

Small teams (<50) may not need it.

If you have 10 people and you all sit in the same room, you probably already know your processes. Go2's value increases with organizational complexity. Below ~50 people, the ROI may not justify the cost.

It requires employee buy-in to work best.

Go2 is best when employees engage with the coaching. If you deploy it secretly or in a punitive context, you'll get data but not the coaching benefits. Transparency about deployment matters.
