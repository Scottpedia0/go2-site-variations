What about privacy? This sounds invasive.

We're not going to insult you by saying "we don't capture much." We capture everything. That's how the AI gets smart enough to actually help.

Frequently Asked Questions

The stuff you're actually wondering.

Do you really capture keystrokes?
Yes. We're not going to lie about it. Keystroke data helps the AI understand workflows at a granular level — what tools you're using, what you're doing in them, where you're getting stuck. But keystroke logs are never shown to managers. They feed the AI. The AI generates coaching. That's it.
What if a worker visits personal sites during work hours?
Go2 will coach the worker directly: "Hey, you might want to refocus." It will never tell their manager what sites they visited or what they typed. The manager might see aggregated focus metrics for the team, but individual browsing data stays between the worker and the AI.
How fast does it start working?
First useful coaching suggestions within hours. Full workflow mapping within a day. People are surprisingly predictable — the AI picks up patterns fast. For complex, multi-step workflows, give it about a week to map everything and suggest optimizations.
What does Go2 actually replace in our stack?
At minimum, your time tracker. Beyond that, Go2 helps you figure out what your tool stack should be — it identifies underused features in tools you already pay for, recommends better alternatives, and maps out automation opportunities. It doesn't automate things for you. It tells you how.
Can managers override the privacy controls?
No. The data governance model is architectural, not policy-based. Raw worker data feeds the AI models. The AI generates aggregated signals. Those signals surface to managers. There's no "admin toggle" to expose raw keystroke logs or browser history. That's by design, not by setting.
Where is our data stored?
Your choice. Go2's cloud, your own cloud infrastructure, or locally. We maintain zero-day retention with AI providers — your data is processed and discarded, never stored by the AI models. SOC 2 compliant across all configurations.
What if my team is small — like 15 people?
That's exactly who we built this for. 15-50 person remote teams where the owner can feel that productivity is leaking but can't pinpoint where. At $15/user/month, a 15-person team is $225/month. The AI typically finds its first automation opportunity within 48 hours.
