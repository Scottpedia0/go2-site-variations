The History of Work Intelligence

For over a century, we've tried to understand how work happens. Each era brought new tools. None brought understanding. Until now.

SCROLL TO BEGIN
1911
The Scientific Management Era

The Stopwatch

Frederick Taylor stood behind workers with a stopwatch, timing every motion. He believed work could be optimized through observation alone. He was half right.

"In the past, the man has been first; in the future, the system must be first." — F.W. Taylor, 1911
1985
The Spreadsheet Era

The Grid

VisiCalc, then Lotus 1-2-3, then Excel. We digitized the ledger but not the understanding. Work became cells and formulas — precise but lifeless. The data was clean. The insight was missing.

Spreadsheets captured what happened. They never captured how or why.
2012
The Dashboard Era

The Dashboard

Tableau, Power BI, Looker. Beautiful visualizations of lagging indicators. We could see what already happened in stunning detail. We still couldn't see what was happening, or what should happen next.

Dashboards are rearview mirrors. You can't drive forward looking backward.
