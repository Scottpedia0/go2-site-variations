The Challenge

Meridian Logistics had been using Time Doctor for three years. While it provided basic time tracking, the company faced several growing problems:

  • Employee resentment: Screenshot monitoring created a surveillance culture. Two senior dispatchers quit citing "feeling like prisoners" in exit interviews.
  • Gaming the system: Employees learned to keep "productive" apps in the foreground while doing other tasks. The data looked good but didn't reflect reality.
  • Overtime crisis: Average overtime was 12.4 hours/week across operations staff, costing $2.1M annually. Management couldn't identify the root cause.
  • Cross-location inconsistency: Dallas operations outperformed Chicago by 34%, but no one understood why — the monitoring data showed similar "activity levels" at both locations.
"We had three years of Time Doctor data and still couldn't answer the basic question: why is Dallas faster than Chicago? The screenshots showed people working. The time logs showed hours. But the actual output told a different story."
David Park
COO, Meridian Logistics

The Solution

In September 2025, Meridian replaced Time Doctor with Go2 across all three offices. The rollout followed Go2's standard implementation playbook:

Week 1

Pilot with Dallas Operations (18 people)

Deployed to the highest-performing office first to establish a "gold standard" baseline.

Week 2

First Insight: Meeting Overload in Chicago

Go2's AI identified that Chicago staff spent 3.2 hours/day in meetings vs. 1.4 in Dallas. This explained the performance gap — not effort, but meeting culture.

Week 3

Expanded to All Three Offices

Full deployment with employee-facing coaching dashboards. Time Doctor uninstalled. Employee response was overwhelmingly positive.

Month 2

Chicago Restructures Meetings

Based on Go2 data, Chicago reduced recurring meetings by 60%. Productivity began closing the gap with Dallas within two weeks.

Month 6

Full Results Measured

47% productivity increase across all offices. Overtime down 40%. Annual savings: $1.2M.

Key Lessons

Lessons Learned

1

Surveillance data can be misleading. Three years of Time Doctor data showed "good" activity levels but masked the real issue: meeting overload consuming productive hours.

2

Coaching beats monitoring. Employee satisfaction jumped from 3.1 to 4.3 after switching from surveillance to coaching, and productivity gains were 3x higher.

3

Start with your best team. Piloting with Dallas (the top performers) set a positive tone and created advocates who championed the rollout to other offices.

4

The problem is rarely effort. Meridian's employees were working hard — they were just working inefficiently. Go2's AI found the systemic issues that raw monitoring never could.
