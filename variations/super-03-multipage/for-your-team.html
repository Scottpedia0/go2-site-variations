What Go2 actually is

In plain language: it's an AI that learns how you work and helps you work smarter.

🤖

A coach, not a camera

Go2 watches your workflow — which tools you use, what tasks you repeat, where you get stuck — and gives you personalized tips to save time. Think of it as having a really smart colleague who notices things you miss.

🎯

It helps YOU, specifically

This isn't generic "productivity training." The AI learns your unique workflow and coaches you on your specific patterns. The tips Sarah gets are different from the tips Jake gets, because they work differently.

🔒

Your manager sees team patterns, not your screen

Your manager gets aggregated insights like "the team spends 34% of time in meetings." They cannot see your individual activity, your screen, or what you do minute-by-minute. This is built into the architecture, not a policy.

💡

You're in control

You can see everything Go2 knows about you at any time. Every data point, every observation. Full transparency. You can also dismiss coaching tips that aren't relevant — the AI learns your preferences.

Questions you probably have

Is this monitoring software?
No. Monitoring software watches you and reports to your boss. Go2 watches your workflow and coaches you directly. Your manager sees team-level patterns, never your individual activity. The difference is fundamental: monitoring is about control, coaching is about improvement.
Can my manager see what websites I visit?
No. Your manager cannot see your browsing history, which sites you visit, or how long you spend on any site. The AI uses this data internally to understand your workflow and generate coaching tips, but it's never exposed to management. Not as raw data, not as summaries, not in any form.
Will it slow down my computer?
No. The agent uses less than 1% of your CPU and minimal memory. It's lighter than most browser extensions. You genuinely won't notice it's there.
Can I turn off the coaching tips?
You can dismiss any tip, and the AI will learn what you find useful over time. Most people keep them on because the tips are genuinely helpful — 89% of workers in our data say so. But you're never forced to interact with them.
What if I have a bad day? Will my manager know?
No. Go2 doesn't track productivity scores, "active time," or anything that would flag a slow day. Your manager sees team-level workflow patterns over weeks, not individual daily performance. Everyone has off days. Go2 doesn't care.
Does it read my messages or emails?
No. Go2 does not capture the content of your messages, emails, or documents. It observes workflow patterns — which tools you switch between, what tasks you repeat — not the content of your work.
What happens to my data if I leave the company?
Your individual data is deleted. The aggregated team patterns (which never identified you personally) remain, but everything tied to you specifically is removed.

Questions? Talk to your manager.

They chose Go2 because it helps the whole team work smarter — including them. If you have concerns, raise them. Transparency goes both ways.
