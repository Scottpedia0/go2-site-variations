I've watched $2 billion in AI spending produce nothing. Here's what nobody tells you.

A letter to every CEO who's been told AI will transform their company.

Dear fellow CEO,

I need to be honest with you about something the consultants won't say and the vendors won't admit. I've spent the last decade in the work intelligence space, and I've watched the AI revolution unfold with equal parts excitement and horror.

The excitement: AI is genuinely transformative. The models are extraordinary. The potential is real.

The horror: Almost nobody is getting value from it.

MIT found 95% of AI pilots fail. BCG found only 5% of companies create real value from AI. This isn't a technology problem. It's a data problem.

Here's the uncomfortable truth that your CTO probably won't tell you at the next board meeting: AI doesn't fail because the models are bad. AI fails because it has absolutely no idea how your company actually works.

Think about it. You buy Claude or ChatGPT or build custom agents. Brilliant technology. Then you point it at your company and say "make us more efficient." And it has no context. No understanding of your processes. No knowledge of the workarounds your team invented. No awareness of the tribal knowledge that lives in Karen's head in accounting.

It's like hiring the smartest person in the world and dropping them into your company blindfolded.

This is the problem we set out to solve. Not by building another AI model. Not by building another dashboard. By building the missing infrastructure that sits between your company and AI.

What we actually built:

  • 1 Cowork.ai — a desktop agent that works alongside every employee. Not watching them. Helping them. Real-time coaching that workers actually love (89% satisfaction). It learns how work happens by being part of the work.
  • 2 The Work Graph — 2M+ worker profiles, process maps, behavioral patterns. This is the data your AI needs. Not what you think happens in your company. What actually happens.
  • 3 Open source agent training tools — We don't replace Claude or ChatGPT. We make them smarter. We sidecar your existing AI tools with the work context they're missing.
  • 4 Automatic process documentation — Every process, documented as it actually happens. Including the three workarounds nobody wrote down and the exception handling that only veteran employees know.

I think of it like Waze. Every driver contributes a little data about road conditions. In return, everyone gets smarter routing. Go2 does the same thing for work. Every worker contributes a little data about how they work. In return, everyone gets smarter tools, better coaching, and AI that actually understands their company.

Workers contribute data, everyone gets smarter. That's the model. That's why 89% of workers love it instead of fighting it.

The surveillance approach — the screenshot-every-5-minutes, keylogger, "productivity score" approach — is a dead end. Workers game it. The data is garbage. Trust evaporates. And you end up with worse intelligence than you started with.

We took the opposite approach. We made something workers actually want to use. When the tool genuinely helps you do your job better, you don't resent it. You rely on it.

I know this sounds too good to be true. So here are the numbers: $60M+ in revenue. 2M+ worker profiles. 89% worker satisfaction. This isn't a pitch deck. This is a company that works.

The question isn't whether AI will transform your company. It's whether you have the infrastructure to make it happen.

If you're spending on AI and not getting results, the problem isn't the AI. The problem is that your AI is flying blind. We built the eyes.

I'd love to show you what we see.
