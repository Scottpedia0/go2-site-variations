Experimental Protocol
The 30-day scientific method
Every claim should be testable. Every result should be measurable. Here's exactly how the experiment runs.
Phase 1
Day 1-3

Baseline Measurement

Go2's lightweight desktop agent deploys silently. No workflow changes. It establishes your team's current operational baseline: how work flows, where time goes, what tools get used.

Phase 2
Day 4-14

Observation Period

AI analyzes patterns using data from ~1M workers. Frontier models (Claude, GPT, Gemini) identify inefficiencies, automation opportunities, and coaching moments specific to your team.

Phase 3
Day 15-28

Treatment Applied

Workers begin receiving real-time AI coaching. Not monitoring. Coaching. "Here's a faster way to do this." Managers get aggregated insight reports. Automation candidates are identified.

Phase 4
Day 29-30

Results Analysis

Before vs. after. Hard numbers. Time saved. Processes streamlined. Automations identified. If the data doesn't show improvement, the experiment failed. No spin. No rationalizing.

Testable Hypotheses
What we expect to prove
These aren't marketing claims. They're hypotheses that the experiment will either confirm or refute with data.
Hypothesis 1

Your team spends 20-35% of time on automatable tasks

Based on data from ~1M workers across similar companies, we predict your team performs significant manual work that could be automated. The experiment will measure the exact percentage.

Hypothesis 2

AI coaching will improve individual productivity by 15-25%

Real-time suggestions from frontier AI (faster tool paths, process shortcuts, automation triggers) should measurably reduce time-to-completion on routine tasks.

Hypothesis 3

At least 3 high-ROI automations will be identified

Every organization we've studied has had automation opportunities hiding in plain sight. We predict at least 3 tasks that, when automated, save 10+ hours per week combined.

Measurement

All results will be objectively verifiable

No vague "feels better." We measure: time-on-task, tool switching frequency, process completion rates, automation opportunities with estimated ROI. Numbers, not narratives.

Measurement

Worker experience will improve, not degrade

We survey workers at day 1 and day 30. If satisfaction doesn't improve (or stays neutral), the coaching model needs adjustment. Workers should feel helped, not watched.

Expected Result

ROI visible before the experiment ends

Based on prior experiments: teams see measurable results by week 3. If we can't demonstrate clear value in 30 days, we don't deserve your business. Period.

Important Variable
Coaching, not surveillance
The experiment only works if workers feel helped, not watched. Here's how the treatment is designed.
Design Principle

Workers receive the coaching

Real-time AI suggestions. "You've been manually exporting this. Here's a one-click method." The coaching comes from frontier AI models trained on data from ~1M workers. It makes the job easier.

Design Principle

Managers receive aggregated insights

No individual surveillance dashboards. Team-level patterns, process bottlenecks, automation opportunities. The view is systemic, not personal. You see the machine, not the people inside it.

Design Principle

The experiment measures systems, not souls

We're testing whether your processes are efficient, not whether your people are "good." Every finding is about the system: tool choices, workflow design, automation gaps. Never about individual performance.
