The Thesis

We've been asking the wrong question

Every boardroom is asking: "How do we deploy AI?" The question assumes the technology is the hard part. It isn't. The hard part is having anything meaningful for AI to work with.

Your company's most valuable asset is institutional knowledge: how deals get closed, how incidents get resolved, how decisions get made under pressure. That knowledge lives in people's heads. No amount of AI spending extracts it.

AI transformation doesn't start with AI. It starts with understanding how work actually happens.

The Bridge

Go2 builds the bridge between human expertise and AI capability

We built Go2 around a simple insight: the best training data for AI agents comes from observing how excellent humans do the work. Not from surveys. Not from consultant interviews. From the actual patterns of real work.

But here's the thing most companies get wrong: you can't observe real work with surveillance tools. People behave differently when they're being watched. The data becomes theater, not truth.

Go2 is worker-first because worker-first is the only way to get data that's actually real.
