The Problem

Your best practices exist.
They're just trapped in individual heads.

Every company builds institutional knowledge over years. But it's fragile — living only in the habits, shortcuts, and instincts of individual employees. When they leave, it leaves with them.

The Departure Problem

Your best account manager quits. Six months of relationship context, pricing nuances, and client preferences walk out the door. The replacement starts from zero.

The Onboarding Gap

New hires take 3–6 months to reach full productivity. Not because they're slow — because the knowledge they need is scattered across dozens of colleagues' heads.

The Blind Spot

The Repetition Tax
