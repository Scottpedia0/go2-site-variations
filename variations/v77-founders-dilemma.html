A letter to founders

"I built this company from nothing. Now it's 30 people and I can't see inside it anymore."

You remember when it was five people. You knew every client by name. You knew who was struggling, who was thriving, and exactly which process was broken because you could feel it. You didn't need dashboards — you were the dashboard.

Then you hired person number twelve. And seventeen. And twenty-three. Somewhere around there, something shifted. Not dramatically — it's not like the company fell apart. It just got... opaque.

Now you walk into standups and nod along. You read weekly reports that tell you everything's fine. And maybe it is. But you don't know anymore. You sense things — a project that's taking too long, a new hire who seems lost, a process that feels inefficient — but you can't point to the exact problem because you're not close enough to the work.

You're too big to know everything personally. Too small for layers of middle management. You're stuck in the gap.

You've thought about hiring a COO. Maybe an operations manager. But you're a 30-person company — adding a $150K management salary to watch 28 people feels wrong. And honestly, the last thing your team needs is another person asking them for status updates.

What you actually need is something impossible: to be in every room again. To see how your best people work so you can help everyone else work that way. To catch problems before they compound. To know — really know — what's happening in your own company.
