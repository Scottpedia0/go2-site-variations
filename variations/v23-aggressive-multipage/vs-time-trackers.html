We coach.

Hubstaff, Time Doctor, ActivTrak, Teramind — they've been around for years. They capture activity. They generate reports. And after all of it, your team is still losing 14 hours a week. Here's why.

Know the field

Here's what they actually do

Each of these tools has a different angle. None of them have generative AI coaching. None of them close the loop from insight to action.

Hubstaff

Time tracking + screenshots
  • Random screenshots every 10 min
  • GPS tracking for field teams
  • Activity levels via mouse/keyboard
  • Basic project time allocation
  • Payroll integration
  • No AI analysis. No coaching.

Time Doctor

Distraction alerts + reports
  • Website/app usage monitoring
  • Distraction alerts (pop-up warnings)
  • Screencasts and screenshots
  • Client/project billing
  • Payroll features
  • No contextual understanding.

ActivTrak

Workforce analytics
  • Application usage dashboards
  • Productivity scoring
  • Team comparison reports
  • Website categorization
  • Location-based insights
  • Analytics only. No intervention.

Teramind

Security + surveillance
  • Keystroke logging
  • Email/chat content monitoring
  • Screen recording (continuous)
  • DLP and insider threat detection
  • Behavioral rules engine
  • Heavy surveillance. Trust-destroying.

The Gap

What none of them can do

These aren't incremental features. They're category-defining capabilities that require generative AI at the core.

Capability gap #1

Generate coaching in context

Every time tracker can tell you "Sarah used Slack for 3 hours." None of them can say "Sarah's Slack usage spikes during her peak focus window — here's a restructure that would recover 6 hours of deep work per week." That requires generative AI. They don't have it.

Capability gap #2

Map and automate workflows

Time trackers record activity. Go2 watches activity long enough to understand the underlying process, map it, identify the repetitive steps, and deploy AI agents to handle them. The tracker shows you a problem. Go2 eliminates it.

Capability gap #3

Coach without surveilling

The entire time tracker category is built on the assumption that managers need to see what individuals do. Go2 is built on the opposite assumption: workers coach themselves when they have the right data. Managers lead with team-level intelligence.
