What Go2 does

A desktop agent that watches work happen, then uses frontier AI to make it happen better.

Go2 sits quietly on each team member's computer. It observes workflows, tool usage, and patterns. Then Claude, GPT, and Gemini analyze what they see -- and coach each person privately, in the moment, with specific suggestions.

Managers get aggregated insights. Not surveillance. Not screenshots. Not keystroke logs. Just clear answers: where is time going, what's working, what could be better, and what should be automated.

What it looks like

No dashboards to check. No reports to read. Go2 comes to you.

  • A quiet notification: "Your team's proposal review step takes 3.2 hours on average. Two people finish in 40 minutes. Want to see their method?" Delivered to the manager
  • A gentle nudge: "You've been in this spreadsheet for 45 minutes. Last time, the formula on row 34 took you 20 minutes to debug. Here's a shortcut." Delivered to the worker, privately
  • A weekly summary: "Your customer support team resolved 12% more tickets this week. The biggest driver: two reps adopted a new triage pattern." Delivered to the team lead
  • An automation flag: "Your invoicing process has 6 manual steps that are identical every time. Estimated savings if automated: 11 hours per week." Delivered to operations
  • A coaching moment: "Top performers on your team check the client portal before every call. You've skipped it the last 4 times. Want a reminder?" Delivered to the individual, privately
How it works

Four steps. All of them quiet.

01

Install

A lightweight desktop agent. Takes about 3 minutes. Runs silently in the background. Your team barely notices it's there.

02

Observe

Go2 watches work patterns -- not content. It understands workflows, tool switches, time allocation, and behavioral patterns across your whole team.

03

Analyze

Three frontier AI models (Claude, GPT, Gemini) cross-reference patterns. They find what your best performers do differently and where automation could save hours.

04

Coach

Each worker gets private, contextual coaching. Managers get aggregated insights. Nobody gets surveillance. The system just makes everyone a little better, every day.

Things you might be wondering

Is this surveillance?
No. Go2 coaches workers privately and gives managers aggregated insights. No screenshots, no keystroke logging, no individual ranking. Workers trust it because it helps them. Managers trust it because honest data produces real results.
Will my team resist it?
Most productivity tools are resisted because they serve management at the worker's expense. Go2 serves both. Workers get private coaching that makes them better at their jobs. That's why our voluntary adoption rate is over 80%.
What AI models does Go2 use?
Claude for nuanced reasoning and coaching. GPT for broad pattern recognition. Gemini for multimodal understanding. Three frontier models, each bringing a different perspective. Together they see patterns no single model could.
How long until we see results?
Quick wins in the first month -- individual coaching tips that save minutes here and there. Systemic patterns by month two -- where time goes, what should be automated. Compound transformation by month three. The quiet kind.
What size team is this for?
Go2 works best for remote teams of 15-50 people. Large enough to have hidden patterns worth discovering. Small enough that each coaching interaction matters.
