Questions

Frequently asked

How does the desktop agent work?
The Go2 desktop agent is a lightweight application (under 50MB) that runs in the background on each employee's computer. It captures workflow patterns including app usage, process steps, and work patterns. All data is encrypted at the device level before transmission. The agent uses minimal system resources (typically less than 2% CPU and 100MB RAM) and can be paused or uninstalled by the employee at any time.
Can managers see individual employee data?
No. Managers see team-level aggregated insights only. Individual coaching tips are delivered directly to each worker and are not visible to managers. This is a core architectural decision, not a configuration option. The system is designed from the ground up to protect individual privacy while providing organizational intelligence.
What's the minimum team size?
Go2 works best with teams of 50 or more. The AI models need sufficient workflow data to generate meaningful insights. For smaller teams, the intelligence value may be limited, though the coaching and automation features can still deliver significant ROI.
How long until we see results?
Most teams see initial workflow maps and insights within the first week. AI coaching begins delivering personalized tips within 2 weeks. Meaningful efficiency improvements are typically measurable within 30 days. AI agent deployment (Transform plan) usually begins at the 4-6 week mark after sufficient training data has been collected.
Is there a free trial?
Yes. Every plan includes a 14-day free trial with full access to all features in that tier. No credit card required to start. We also offer a personalized demo where we'll show you what Go2 would surface specifically for your operations.
Can I change plans later?
Absolutely. You can upgrade or downgrade at any time. When upgrading, the new features are available immediately and billing is prorated. When downgrading, the change takes effect at the start of your next billing cycle and all your data is retained according to the new plan's retention policy.
