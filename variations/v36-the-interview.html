The AI That Coaches Your Workforce (Not Surveils Them)

We sat down with Go2's founding team to ask the hard questions about AI-powered workforce optimization. Here's the unedited conversation.

12 min read · March 2026

So what is Go2, in plain English?

Go2 is a lightweight desktop agent that sits on your employees' computers. It observes how work actually happens — the apps they use, the workflows they follow, the patterns in their day — and feeds all of that into frontier AI models like Claude, GPT, and Gemini.

Those models do two things: they coach each worker individually with personalized tips and workflow suggestions, and they give managers aggregated team-level insights about bottlenecks, process gaps, and automation opportunities.

Think of it as giving every employee their own AI productivity coach, and giving every manager an AI-powered view into how their team actually works — without ever turning it into surveillance.

Wait — you capture everything? Screens, keystrokes, audio? Isn't that creepy?

I understand why that's the first reaction. And honestly, it should be the first question. Here's the critical distinction: workers see their own data. Every coaching tip, every workflow observation, every insight — the employee has full access to everything Go2 knows about them.

Managers? They see aggregated team insights only. Never individual surveillance. Never a screenshot of someone's screen. Never a log of someone's keystrokes. The AI synthesizes everything into team-level patterns: "Your design team spends 4.2 hours per week on manual file naming that could be automated." Not "Sarah was on Instagram at 2:47pm."

The data capture isn't the product — the intelligence that comes out of it is the product. We capture comprehensively so the AI can coach precisely. That's it.

The old model was: capture data, show it to the boss, let them punish. Our model is: capture data, teach the AI, let it help everyone.

How is this different from Hubstaff or Time Doctor?

They watch. We coach. That's the fundamental difference, and it changes everything.

Hubstaff, Time Doctor, Teramind — those tools capture screenshots and activity data and hand it to managers in a dashboard. The implicit message to employees is: we don't trust you. Those tools have zero generative AI. None. They just record and report.

Go2 feeds everything into Claude, GPT, and Gemini — the most capable AI models on the planet — and generates actual, actionable coaching for every individual worker. The employee gets tips like:

  • "You switch between Slack and your spreadsheet 47 times per day — here's a workflow that cuts that in half"
  • "Your email drafts take 3x longer on Mondays — consider batching them Tuesday morning when your response time is fastest"
  • "This repetitive copy-paste sequence can be automated with a two-line macro"

No competitor does this. Not one. We're the only platform with generative AI coaching, and we've been building on 7+ years of workforce data from nearly a million workers to train our understanding of productivity patterns.

What does an employee actually experience day-to-day?

The agent runs quietly in the background. No pop-ups, no interruptions, no performance anxiety. Employees almost forget it's there — until they get their first coaching insight.

Here's what a typical week looks like for a Go2 user:

  • Monday morning: A brief digest surfaces their top 3 productivity patterns from last week, with one specific suggestion to try
  • Mid-week: A workflow tip appears — "You've been manually formatting these reports. Here's a template that saves ~45 minutes per batch"
  • Friday: A weekly summary shows time saved, skills growing, and areas where they're already improving

It's like having a thoughtful mentor who's been watching your work and quietly building up observations, then sharing them at exactly the right moment. Employees start asking for more tips. We've seen engagement rates above 70% on coaching recommendations — people actually implement the suggestions.

Okay, so what do I see as the manager?

You see the forest, never the trees. Your dashboard shows team-level patterns that would be invisible otherwise:

  • Process bottlenecks: "Your sales team spends 6.8 hours per week on data entry that could be automated with a CRM integration"
  • Workflow gaps: "Three different team members use three different processes for client onboarding — standardizing would save 12 hours per week"
  • Automation opportunities: "These 5 repetitive tasks across your team are strong candidates for automation, ranked by time savings"
  • Skill development signals: "Your team's proficiency with [Tool X] has increased 34% this quarter — the coaching is working"

What you never see: individual screenshots, personal browsing data, keystroke logs, or anything that could be used for surveillance. The AI does the looking. You get the wisdom.

You don't need to see what Sarah did at 2:47pm. You need to know that your team loses 23 hours a week to a broken handoff process — and here's exactly how to fix it.

I need to ask about data. Where does it go? Who can see it? How is it protected?

Good — you should be asking this. Here's the full picture:

Data in transit: Everything is encrypted with TLS 1.3. Data moves from the desktop agent to our processing infrastructure through encrypted channels. Nothing is transmitted in plaintext, ever.

Data at rest: AES-256 encryption. Industry standard for financial and healthcare data. Your workforce data gets the same protection as bank records.

Access controls: Employees see their own data. Managers see aggregated team insights only. Our AI models process the raw data but the raw data itself is never exposed to managers. There's a hard architectural boundary — this isn't a policy, it's how the system is built.

Data retention: You control it. Set your own retention windows. Delete anytime. When you delete, it's gone — not archived, not retained "just in case." Gone.

Compliance: We're built for teams that care about this. SOC 2 practices, GDPR-ready data handling, and we'll work with your legal team on any specific requirements.

The short version: your data is yours. We process it to generate intelligence. We never sell it, share it, or use it for anything beyond making your team better.
