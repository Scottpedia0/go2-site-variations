Instructions

Preheat Your Organization

Before you begin, set the right temperature. Communicate to your team that Go2 is a coaching tool, not a monitoring tool. It captures workflow patterns (which apps, in what order, for how long) — not keystrokes, not screenshots, not content. Think of it as a fitness tracker for work, not a surveillance camera. Let this message marinate for at least a day.

Many recipes fail at this step. If your team thinks "surveillance," resistance will curdle the entire dish. Transparency is the key ingredient. Show them the employee dashboard — they can see exactly what Go2 captures about them.

Install the Desktop Agent

Download Cowork.ai from go2.io and install on 5-50 desks. This takes approximately 3 minutes per workstation. No IT infrastructure changes required. No servers to deploy. No APIs to configure. Just install and let it simmer. The agent will begin capturing work patterns immediately, like a slow cooker that starts working the moment you plug it in.

Start with your most process-heavy team. Operations, finance, and customer success tend to produce the richest flavor of automatable workflows.

Let It Observe (24 Hours)

Don't touch anything. Don't configure anything. Don't try to optimize anything. Just let Go2 watch how your team actually works. Within 24 hours, you'll receive your first insights. These will include workflow maps that show the real process (not the one in the handbook), context-switching patterns, and your team's natural productivity rhythms. This is the base stock of your AI implementation — everything else builds on it.

Fold In the Coaching Layer

After 24-48 hours of observation, Go2's AI coaching activates automatically. Workers will receive gentle, contextual tips: "You've switched between Slack and your IDE 8 times in 12 minutes — try a 25-minute focus block." These tips should be folded in gently, not forced. The 89% acceptance rate proves the batter rises naturally when the advice is actually useful.

Don't mandate tip acceptance. The best results come when workers voluntarily adopt suggestions because they see immediate benefits. Trust the process — this isn't a recipe you can rush.

Identify Automation Opportunities (Week 1-2)

As the coaching data accumulates, Go2 will begin flagging repetitive processes that are ripe for automation. Think of these as the low-hanging fruit — the tasks that are done the same way, dozens of times a day, by multiple people. Go2 ranks them by estimated time savings and automation confidence. Pick the ripest one first.

Slow-Cook the Process Model (Days 14-60)

This is where patience matters most. Go2 needs to observe enough instances of a process to build a reliable model — including edge cases, workarounds, and the tribal knowledge that exists only in your team's heads. For a process like invoice handling, this typically means observing 500-3,000 instances. You can't rush a good ragu, and you can't rush a good process model.

The "consultant shortcut" of interviewing people about their process is like using instant roux instead of making it from scratch. It'll technically create something, but it won't have the depth of flavor (data) that makes the dish (agent) actually work.

Deploy in Shadow Mode (Day 60-62)

Once Go2 has a confident process model, deploy the AI agent in shadow mode. This means the agent processes the same work as your humans, in parallel, without taking any action. Compare the agent's decisions against the human's decisions. When agreement exceeds 98-99%, the agent is ready. Think of shadow mode as a taste test before serving.

Promote to Active and Serve

Promote the agent from shadow to active. Set up the human review queue for low-confidence decisions. Serve immediately and watch as your team's time is freed up for strategic, creative, high-value work. Garnish with a Go2-generated ROI report to impress the executives.

The people whose tasks get automated don't lose their jobs — they get better ones. Sarah went from processing invoices to leading vendor strategy. That's the garnish on this dish.

Repeat for Every Process

Each successful automation makes the next one easier. The data flywheel spins faster. Your team trusts Go2 more. Leadership sees ROI and approves expansion. Before you know it, you're running a fully automated kitchen — with humans as the master chefs directing AI sous-chefs.

Substitutions & Variations

No executive buy-in? Start with a free trial. Let the results speak for themselves. Buy-in comes naturally when savings are real.

Small team (<10)? Go2 works great with small teams. Fewer people often means more tribal knowledge concentrated in fewer heads — making capture even more valuable.

Remote team? Go2's desktop agent works anywhere. It doesn't matter if your kitchen is in Chicago or Chiang Mai.

Tempted to add consultants? Don't. Seriously. Every consulting dollar spent on process mapping is a dollar that could be spent on letting Go2 observe the actual process. The results are categorically better.
