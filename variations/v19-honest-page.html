Why capture everything

The AI argument for total data collection.

Here's the hard truth about AI coaching: garbage in, garbage out. Every productivity tool that captures "just enough" data gives you generic advice. "You should try time-blocking." Thanks.

Go2 captures everything because that's the only way AI can give specific, useful coaching. Not "try using keyboard shortcuts" but "you copy data from Salesforce into that spreadsheet 14 times a day — here's a Zapier automation that does it in zero."

The AI sees the whole picture: what tools you use, how you move between them, where you lose time, what meetings accomplish nothing, which workflows are broken. It can't see any of that from a time tracker that knows you spent "3 hours in Chrome."

"Can't you get good insights from less data?"
No. We've tried. Partial data gives you partial insights. When you capture app usage without keystrokes, you know someone spent 2 hours in Excel but not whether they were doing analysis or manually copying rows from another sheet. The keystroke data is what turns vague observations into specific, actionable coaching. We understand this makes some people uncomfortable. That's a reasonable reaction. We think the results justify it.

Who sees what

The data governance model, in plain language.

This is where most companies hand-wave. We won't. Here's exactly who sees what:

The Worker

Sees: Their own full data. Personal AI coaching. Specific suggestions.

Example: "You spent 40 minutes reformatting that report. Here's a template macro."

The Manager

Sees: Aggregated team insights. Workflow patterns. Recommended actions.

Does NOT see: Keystrokes. Browser histories. Personal messages. Screenshots.

Think of it this way: AI talks to AI without revealing the raw data underneath. The system might tell a worker "you should spend less time on personal browsing during work hours." It will never tell their manager what they were browsing.

"How do we know managers can't access raw data?"
Permissions are enforced at the platform level, not the policy level. Managers literally cannot see raw keystroke logs or browser histories in the interface — there's no button, no export, no workaround. Your company can choose to give designated analysts deeper access for compliance purposes. That's a company decision, not a default. SOC 2 certified. Data can live on your infrastructure if you want.

What the AI actually does

The product, without the hype.

Go2's AI does one thing: it watches how your team works and tells them how to work better. Specifically:

It finds wasted effort. Repetitive copy-paste workflows. Manual data entry that could be automated. Meetings where the same information gets repeated. Reports that take 2 hours but could take 10 minutes with the right template.

It coaches directly. Workers get proactive suggestions in plain language. Not a dashboard with charts — actual advice. "You use Intercom but haven't enabled the AI auto-reply feature. Here's how. It would handle about 30% of your support tickets."

It maps your workflows. Go2 builds a data science taxonomy underneath your organization automatically — patterns, relationships, bottlenecks — without anyone on your team lifting a finger.

It does NOT automate anything itself. It tells you what to automate and how. It identifies that you're doing the same thing 50 times a day and shows you the fix. Whether you fix it yourself or use our white-glove service to set it up, that's your call.

"How fast does this actually work?"
Coaching suggestions can start within hours. People are more predictable than they think. For complex, multi-step workflows, give it a day. The AI processes 20-50K tokens of workflow data per person per day — batched weekly for deeper pattern analysis. Past a few weeks, the suggestions get very specific. We won't pretend it's magic on day one. But it's useful on day one.

Questions you should be asking

And our honest answers.

Some might, initially. The key is how you roll it out. If you install it silently and workers find out later, yes, they'll hate it and you'll deserve it. If you're transparent — "we're installing Go2, it captures everything, the AI coaches you personally and your manager only sees aggregated data" — most people are fine with it. Our 91% worker satisfaction score comes from environments where everyone knows exactly what's happening. Transparency isn't just our policy. It has to be yours too.
Can I try it before committing?
Yes. Start with a small team for 30 days. You'll have coaching suggestions within hours and meaningful workflow data within a week. If it doesn't demonstrate clear value in 30 days, leave. No contracts on the monthly plan.
What if our team works fine without this?
Then you probably don't need us. Go2 is for teams where there's a clear gap between how productive people could be and how productive they are. If your team is already crushing it with well-optimized workflows and strong tool adoption, spending $39/user/month on coaching they don't need would be a waste of money. We'd rather lose a sale than have an unhappy customer.
What happens to our data if we leave?
You get a full export. We delete everything on our end. If data lives on your infrastructure, you already have it. Standard stuff, but worth stating.
Is this just repackaged employee monitoring?
The capture layer, taken alone, is employee monitoring. We won't pretend otherwise. What makes Go2 different is everything that happens after capture: AI processing, direct worker coaching, aggregated (not raw) manager insights, workflow mapping, tool optimization. If you strip away the AI layer, you'd have a monitoring tool. That's not what we sell and not what customers pay for. But we won't gaslight you about what's underneath.
