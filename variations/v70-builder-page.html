We spent 7 years placing workers. We saw the same waste in every company. We built Go2 to fix it.

What We Believe

The principles behind Go2

Coaching, never surveillance

We've seen what monitoring does to trust. Go2 coaches workers privately and gives managers aggregated insights. Nobody gets ranked. Nobody gets screenshots taken.

Built from the bottom up

Most tools impose processes top-down. Go2 discovers what already works and amplifies it. Your best practices, captured and spread. Not our theory of how you should work.

Workers should benefit too

If workers don't trust the tool, the data is garbage. Go2 helps each person get better at their job. When workers benefit, the data is honest and the results are real.

Domain expertise matters

You can't build a great workforce tool without understanding workforce operations. We spent 7 years in the trenches. That experience is baked into every feature.

Frequently Asked

Questions we get from founders like you

How is Go2 different from Time Doctor, Hubstaff, or Teramind?

Those tools monitor and report. Go2 coaches and improves. Monitoring makes people defensive -- they game the metrics, minimize the tracker, feel watched. Coaching makes people better. Workers voluntarily adopt Go2 at over 80% because it actually helps them individually. The data you get is higher quality because nobody's gaming it.

Can my team see what the AI sees about them?

Workers see their own coaching tips. Managers see aggregated team patterns. Nobody sees individual surveillance data. We designed it this way because we've placed hundreds of thousands of workers and we know what happens when trust breaks down: everything breaks down.

What if we only have 15-20 people?

That's actually the sweet spot. Small enough that each person's improvement is visible. Large enough that patterns across the team are meaningful. Most of our best results come from teams of 15-50 remote workers.

Why three AI models instead of one?

Each model brings a different strength. Claude excels at nuanced coaching conversations. GPT is strong at broad pattern recognition across large datasets. Gemini adds multimodal understanding. Together they catch things no single model would see. We learned this from years of workforce data -- no single perspective captures the full picture.

How quickly will we see results?

Individual coaching tips start within the first two weeks. Team-level patterns and automation mapping emerge by week four. The compound effect -- where everything starts reinforcing everything else -- typically hits around month three. By month six, teams tell us they can't imagine working without it.
