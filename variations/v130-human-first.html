The Human-First Manifesto

For two decades, "productivity software" has meant one thing: surveillance. Screenshots every 5 minutes. Keystroke logging. Mouse movement tracking. Software that treats adults like suspects.

The result? Workers hate these tools. They game the metrics. They burn out. They quit. The very act of watching destroys the thing you're trying to measure.

We asked a different question: What if a productivity tool was something workers actually wanted to use?

Go2 is an AI coach. It doesn't report to your boss. It reports to you. It learns your patterns, helps you find your flow, protects your focus time, and nudges you when you're drifting. It's like having a world-class productivity coach in your pocket.

The beautiful irony? When you build for the worker, the company wins too. 23% average productivity gains across 47,000 coached workers. Not through surveillance. Through genuine human development.
