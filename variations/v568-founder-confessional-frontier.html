A Moment of Vulnerability

I'll never forget that night. It was 2 AM, and I was staring at my laptop screen, surrounded by empty coffee cups and crumpled notes. My startup had grown from a solo operation in my garage to a team of 50 passionate people scattered across time zones. We were hitting milestones, closing deals, and the revenue was climbing. But deep down, I felt like I was flying blind.

How was work really getting done? Were my team members burning out behind their screens? Why did some projects drag on forever while others flew? I had no visibility. No real insights. Just gut feelings and endless meetings that felt like guessing games. I remember thinking, "Am I even a good leader? Or am I just pretending?"

"The fear of not knowing was eating me alive. Trust is everything, but without data, it's just hope."

As an SMB owner, you're the captain of the ship, but what if the fog is so thick you can't see the horizon? I tried everything—spreadsheets, check-ins, even those clunky time-tracking tools that felt more like Big Brother than a helping hand. They monitored, sure, but they didn't coach. They didn't help us get better. And let's be honest, no one wants to feel watched. I didn't want surveillance; I wanted empowerment.
