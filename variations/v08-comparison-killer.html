FAQ

Questions switchers always ask

How is Go2 different from just another time tracker?

Time trackers measure what happened. Go2 does that too, but then uses generative AI to coach each person on how to work better. No other product in this category has AI coaching — not Hubstaff, not Time Doctor, not ActivTrak, not Teramind. They give managers data. We give workers improvement.

Won't my team push back on being monitored?

That's the whole point. Go2 doesn't take screenshots, record screens, or log keystrokes. Workers get their own private AI coach with tips tailored to their work style. Managers see aggregated team-level insights, not individual surveillance feeds. Teams actually want to use Go2 because it helps them, not watches them.

What does the AI coaching actually look like?

It's specific and actionable. Instead of "be more productive," Go2 says things like "Your deepest focus happens before 11am but you have meetings scheduled then 3 days this week. Moving those to the afternoon could give you 4 more hours of uninterrupted work." It's based on each person's real behavioral data.

We're already paying for Hubstaff/Time Doctor. Is it worth switching?

At $15/user/month, Go2 costs about the same as the mid-tier plans from competitors — but delivers an entirely different category of value. You keep everything you already get (activity tracking, reports, dashboards) and add AI coaching, workflow mapping, and aggregated insights that no competitor offers at any price.

What do managers see vs. what workers see?

Workers see their own AI coaching: personalized tips, workflow suggestions, productivity insights about their own patterns. Managers see aggregated team dashboards: team-level trends, workflow bottlenecks, and optimization opportunities. No individual surveillance data flows to managers.

Does it work for remote teams?

Go2 was built for remote and hybrid teams. It's especially valuable when managers can't observe work directly — instead of resorting to surveillance tools, Go2 gives each person their own AI coach and gives managers visibility into team-level patterns.
