A letter from our founder

Why I built Go2

I've spent 7 years in the staffing industry. I've placed thousands of workers. And the thing I keep seeing is this: good people, wasting hours every day on things that shouldn't take that long.

Not because they're lazy. Because nobody can see the waste.

A data entry person spending 40 minutes on something that should take 10 — not because they're slow, but because nobody ever showed them the shortcut. A project manager copying numbers between three spreadsheets every Monday morning because "that's how we've always done it." A new hire figuring things out alone because the training doc is from 2019.

I watched this happen across hundreds of companies. Thousands of workers. And I realized something: the problem isn't people. The problem is visibility.

If you can't see how work actually gets done, you can't improve it. You're just guessing.

I built Go2 to make it visible.

Go2 sits on your team's computers. It watches how they actually work — not to spy, but to understand. Then AI (Claude, GPT, Gemini — the best models in the world) coaches each person individually. "Use this shortcut." "Automate this report." "Here's how your fastest colleague handles this."

Your team gets better at their jobs. You see where the hours go. Nobody gets a surveillance report.

We've seen it across roughly a million workers now. The pattern is always the same: people aren't underperforming. They're under-supported. Give them the right nudge at the right time, and everything changes.

That's it. That's Go2.

If you run a team of 15 to 50 people and you've ever wondered where the time goes — I'd love to show you.

Scott Moran

Founder, Go2
