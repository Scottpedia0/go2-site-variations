01

We've Been Measuring the Wrong Things

For decades, we've measured productivity like factory output: hours at desk, keystrokes per minute, apps opened, mouse movements. We turned knowledge work into an assembly line and then wondered why creativity died.

The surveillance industry grew to $4.5 billion by selling a comforting lie: if you can see what people are doing, you can make them do more.

It doesn't work. It never worked. And deep down, you know this.

"The most productive person on your team looks idle to a screenshot tool. They're thinking. That's their job."
02

Surveillance Destroys What It Claims to Protect

Every screenshot taken is a message: "We don't trust you." Every keystroke logged says: "Your judgment doesn't matter."

The research is unambiguous. Employee monitoring reduces creative output by 40%. Surveilled workers are 2.5x more likely to quit. The tool meant to increase productivity is the single biggest destroyer of it.

The Old Way

  • Screenshot every 5 minutes
  • Track keystrokes and mouse moves
  • Monitor app usage second-by-second
  • Record screens continuously
  • Score "productivity" by activity

The Way Forward

  • Understand work patterns, not surveillance
  • Coach, don't police
  • Aggregate insights, not individual spying
  • Protect privacy as a feature
  • Measure outcomes, not keystrokes
"If your productivity tool makes people nervous, it's not a productivity tool. It's a fear tool."
03

Understanding > Measuring

The question isn't "How many hours did they work?" It's "Are they able to do their best work?"

Are meetings eating their focus time? Is context-switching fragmenting their deep work? Are they burning out because nobody noticed the warning signs? These are the questions that matter.

You don't need a surveillance camera in the office. You need a coach who understands how work actually happens.

04

Our Five Principles

1. Privacy Is Non-Negotiable

No screenshots. No keylogging. No screen recording. Ever. If your productivity insights require violating privacy, your insights are worthless.

2. Coaching Beats Policing

People improve when they're helped, not watched. AI coaching delivers 42% improvement. Surveillance delivers resentment.

3. Transparency Goes Both Ways

If you can see your team's data, your team should see it too. Employees own their productivity data. Full stop.

4. Patterns Matter More Than Points

A single day means nothing. Trends mean everything. We look at patterns over time, not snapshots in moments.

5. The Goal Is Human Flourishing

Productivity isn't about extracting more from people. It's about removing the barriers that prevent great work from happening.
