What actually breaks when you scale

01

Knowledge stays locked in heads

Your first 10 employees know how everything works. Employee #50 has no idea why anything is done a certain way.

02

Onboarding becomes a bottleneck

Every new hire needs weeks of hand-holding. Your best people spend more time teaching than producing.

03

Process quality degrades

The careful way things were done at 20 people becomes the chaotic way at 80. Standards slip because nobody wrote them down.

04

Communication overhead explodes

Meetings multiply. Slack channels proliferate. More time is spent coordinating work than doing work.

05

Culture becomes a rumor

"The way we do things here" used to be felt. Now it needs to be taught — and nobody's teaching it.

06

Founders become bottlenecks

Decisions still flow through 2-3 people. They become the constraint on the company's growth.
