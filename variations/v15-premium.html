Questions

What leaders ask before they commit

How is this different from time tracking? +
Time trackers measure hours. Go2 understands work. We capture the full context of how tasks move through your organization — which tools, which handoffs, which bottlenecks — and then use AI to coach individuals, optimize teams, and eventually automate entire workflows with agents. A time tracker is a stopwatch. Go2 is a strategist.
Will my employees feel surveilled? +
The opposite. Go2's coaching engine gives workers a personal AI advisor that helps them work smarter — better focus patterns, tool suggestions, workflow shortcuts. In our data, teams using Go2 report higher satisfaction because the platform invests in them, not just monitors them. Privacy controls are granular and transparent.
What does "agent deployment" actually mean? +
Once Go2 maps a workflow in detail — the inputs, decisions, outputs, and edge cases — it can build an AI agent that executes that workflow autonomously. Think: invoice processing, data entry, report generation, customer onboarding steps. The agent is trained on how your best people actually do the work, not on generic templates.
Why is Go2 more expensive than alternatives? +
Because it does more. A $6/user time tracker gives you screenshots and bar charts. Go2 gives you AI coaching, workflow intelligence, cross-team pattern detection, and agent deployment. The question isn't "is it cheap?" — it's "does the 4.2x ROI justify the investment?" For every client so far, the answer has been yes within the first quarter.
How long does deployment take? +
Most teams are capturing data within 48 hours. Meaningful coaching insights begin at week two. Workflow mapping matures over 30-60 days as the system learns your patterns. Agent deployment readiness typically surfaces around day 60. Business and Enterprise plans include a dedicated success manager who guides the entire rollout.
